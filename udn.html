Union of Democratic Nations (UDN)


  • The Union of Democratic Nations exists in a constant pull between its two stronger neighbors.
  • It is a well developed nation with a well educated population. However, it lacks abundant natural resources, is smaller in territory then its neighbors, and has a much smaller population of 15 million.
  • The UDN is more technologically advanced than its neighbors in many areas, but has not heavily invested in space. Its technology has been focused on food production.
  • While technologically savvy, the UDN is nowhere near as wealthy as its neighbors.
  • The UDN would be last to colonize Mars; its mission will reach Mars in ten years. Its space program is focused on unmanned science missions.
  • The UDN's goal is to preserve the ability to colonize in the future.
  • Secondary Goals Include:
    1. Maintenance of outer-space treaty; no property rights and all commercial and colonizing development must first be approved by the international community
    2. Scientific research; preservation and limited colonization until preliminary findings are satisfactorily carried out.
    3. Demilitarization or limited military presence in space.
